The Golden Age


The superhero always defeats the villain.
Clear stylistic distinction between good and evil.
Basic sequential images (close to comic strips in newspapers) and straightforward stories.
The cartooning was simple.
Basic square grid, often more text than images

Superman
Wonderwoman
Captain America

The Silver Age


Historic events (f.e. World War II) called for a change in storytelling.
Publishers explored other genres such as horror.
Visual styles mimed the themes.
An era of artistic experimentation and political supression started as a result of the CCA censorship.
Cover images became an artistic represantation of an issues theme/protoganist's state of mind.
Took inspiration from past art movements such as Surrealism.

Hulk
Flash
Fantastic Four

The Bronze Age


CCA censorship ends.
The comics dealt with realistic stories/stories of urban life that had no real answer to them.
Photorealistic depictions of the urban landscape.
Alter ego of superheroes and everyday people get in the focus.
Depth of focus and lighting, gave comics a cinematic style.

Spiderman
X Men
Punisher
Iron Man
Green Lantern

The Dark Age


Characters were psychological complex, often dangerously.The line between hero and villain disappeared.
The style got darker and plays with lightig, long shadows and deep dark contrasting colors.
The night was the main setting.
Took inspiration from noir film and the silver age in sense of unnatural angles and disturbing imagery.

Watchmen
Joker
Sin City
Spawn

The Ageless Age


Can't be catogorized.
Advanced technologies has led to a broad range of illustrative techniques (3D modelling, digital painting etc.) Some stories are even adapted to motion comics.
Styles vary drastically depending on nature of the story and choices of the creator.
Line between film and comics is thin.

Walking Dead
Chew
Saga
Scott Pilgrim
